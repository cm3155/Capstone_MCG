          William Barr and Barbara Comstock credit technology with greatly lowering costs for the retail investor
          ("Gary Gensler's Plan to Control Information," op-ed, Sept. 11). While that is certainly a factor, I think the
          lion's share of the credit goes to Charles Schwab and his namesake firm in brokerage as well as Jack Bogle
          and his Vanguard mutual funds.                                          
          Technology and economies of scale are enablers, but those two leaders blazed the trail, to the chagrin of
          many competitors, to slash fees. Despite the technology, downward price trends have been more muted in
          other areas, such as hedge funds.                                       
          Martin Riehl                                                            
          Downingtown, Pa.
