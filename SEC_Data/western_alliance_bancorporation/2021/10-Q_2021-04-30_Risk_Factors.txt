 Item 1A. Risk Factors. 

The Company has updated the risk factors contained in Item 1A of its Annual Report on Form 10-K for the year ended December 31, 2020 based on the completion of the Company&#8217;s acquisition of AHM on April 7, 2021. Other than as set forth below, there were no material changes to the risk factors disclosed in Item 1A of the Annual Report on Form 10-K for the year ended December 31, 2020. 

The risk factor captioned &#8220; The Company&#8217;s proposed acquisition of AHM is subject to regulatory approvals and other closing conditions and may be more difficult, costly or time-consuming to complete than the Company expects &#8221; is no longer applicable. 

The risk factor captioned &#8220; If the Merger is completed, the addition of AHM&#8217;s national mortgage franchise would present risks that could cause the Company to not realize the strategic and financial goals contemplated at the time it entered into the agreement to acquire AHM or otherwise adversely affect the Company&#8217;s results of operations &#8221; has been updated as follows: 

The recent addition of AHM&#8217;s national mortgage franchise presents risks that could cause the Company to not realize the strategic and financial goals of the AHM acquisition or could otherwise adversely affect the Company&#8217;s results of operations . 

Risks the Company faces with respect to its recently completed acquisition of AHM include: 

&#8226; Management&#8217;s estimates regarding AHM&#8217;s future earnings potential may not be achievable, because AHM&#8217;s performance could be adversely impacted by a rising rate environment, changes in the mix of purchase versus refinancing volumes, competition, or other factors not known or anticipated by the Company; 

&#8226; The integration of AHM into WAB may be more costly or time-consuming than expected despite the fact that AHM continues to operate under its existing brand and management team; 

&#8226; The Company may not realize the benefits it expects to achieve from the acquisition of AHM such as those anticipated from funding, cross-selling, and other integration synergies; 

&#8226; The Company is subject to increased compliance costs and risks with respect to aspects of AHM&#8217;s business that differ from or are larger in scope than WAB&#8217;s previously existing similar operations, including: 

&#9702; The need to maintain various state licenses and federal and government-sponsored agency approvals required to conduct AHM&#8217;s business, and the risk of adverse consequences resulting from periodic examinations by such state and federal agencies or from changes in laws or regulations that may be promulgated in the future; 

&#9702; Increased compliance risk and cost associated with federal, state and local laws, regulations and judicial and administrative decisions relating to mortgage loans and consumer protection, including those designed to discourage predatory lending, collections and servicing practices with respect to mortgage loans; and 

&#9702; Increased compliance risk and costs associated with federal, state and local laws related to data privacy and the handling of non-public personal financial information of AHM&#8217;s customers, including the California Consumer Protection Act and similar regulations that have been or may be enacted by other states; 

&#8226; The Company may have difficulties retaining key personnel from AHM or managing AHM&#8217;s technology platform; 

&#8226; The Company&#8217;s operating results may be adversely impacted by claims or liabilities related to AHM&#8217;s business including, among others, (i) claims from government agencies, current or former customers or employees, consumers, financing providers, vendors and other business partners or third parties; (ii) repurchase and indemnification obligations with respect to sold loans or any failure to be able to enforce repurchase and indemnification obligations of counterparties with respect to purchased loans; and (iii) counterparty and interest rate risk with respect to derivative and hedging instruments; 

&#8226; AHM&#8217;s business may be further affected by the continuation or worsening of the COVID-19 pandemic; and 

&#8226; AHM&#8217;s business may be adversely impacted by changes in the competitive or regulatory landscape.
